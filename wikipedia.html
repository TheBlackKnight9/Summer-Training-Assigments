Lenovo Group Limited, often shortened to Lenovo (/lɛˈnoʊvoʊ/ lə-NOH-voh), is a Chinese multinational technology company specializing in designing, manufacturing, and marketing consumer electronics, personal computers, software, business solutions, and related services. Lenovo is the world's largest personal computer vendor by unit sales as of 2023. The company also produces tablets, smartphones, workstations, servers, supercomputers, electronic storage devices, IT management software, and smart televisions.

Lenovo was founded in Beijing in 1984 as Legend by a team of engineers led by Liu Chuanzhi. Initially, the company was primarily a distributor of imported computers but began developing a circuit board that would allow IBM-compatible personal computers to process Chinese characters in 1990. In 2005, Lenovo acquired IBM's personal computer business, including the ThinkPad laptop and tablet lines. Lenovo has operations in over 60 countries and sells its products in around 180 countries. The company has dual headquarters in Beijing, China, and Morrisville, North Carolina, United States.

Lenovo was founded on 1 November 1984 in Beijing as "Legend" by Liu Chuanzhi with a group of ten engineers from the Chinese Academy of Sciences. The company received ¥200,000 in start-up capital from the Academy. The company initially focused on distributing imported computers, but soon shifted to developing its own hardware. The company's initial business was the resale of televisions as well as magnetically encoded cards and eventually magnetic card readers.

In 1990, Lenovo started developing its own personal computers, and by 1994 had gained significant market share in China. The company renamed its PC brand from "Legend" to "Lenovo" in 2003 to expand overseas. The name "Lenovo" is a portmanteau of "Le-" (from Legend) and "novo" (Latin for "new"). The company officially changed its name to Lenovo in 2004.

IBM acquisition and expansion

Lenovo achieved international recognition when it acquired IBM's Personal Computing Division in 2005 for $1.25 billion, gaining the ThinkPad laptop line and making it the third-largest computer maker worldwide. This acquisition significantly increased Lenovo's market share and provided access to IBM's high-end corporate clients. The deal included the ThinkPad laptop and tablet lines, the ThinkCentre desktop line, and the right to use the IBM brand for five years.

Following the IBM acquisition, Lenovo announced in 2011 the formation of a joint venture with NEC to create NEC Lenovo Japan Group to boost their market share in Japan. In 2012, Lenovo acquired the Brazilian electronics company CCE, and in 2014, it purchased IBM's x86 server business for $2.1 billion. That same year, Lenovo also acquired Motorola Mobility from Google for $2.91 billion, expanding its smartphone business.

Recent developments

In recent years, Lenovo has continued to expand its product portfolio and global presence. The company has maintained its position as the world's largest PC manufacturer since 2013, with a market share of approximately 24% as of 2023. Lenovo has increasingly focused on developing high-performance computing solutions, artificial intelligence capabilities, and expanding into the Internet of Things (IoT) market.

In 2018, Lenovo became the largest provider of supercomputers on the TOP500 list by number of systems. The company continues to invest in research and development, with innovation centers in Beijing, Yokohama, and Morrisville. In 2020, amid the COVID-19 pandemic, Lenovo reported strong growth in its PC business as remote work and education drove increased demand for its products.
